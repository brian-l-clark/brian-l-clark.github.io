Business unit detail

ROP — Corporate-owned locations

  • Brands: Grease Monkey, SpeeDee, Uncle Ed's, American Lube Fast, Kwik Kar, Herbert Automotive, Economy Oil
  • Grease Monkey: Mix of ROP and franchise — roughly half corporate, half franchisee
  • SpeeDee: Predominantly franchise with a small number of ROP locations
  • Agency: L3 for all ROP digital. ADI (YouTech) manages Colorado ROP.
  • Channels: Full suite — Search (Brand + NonBrand), PMax, Demand Gen, YouTube, iHeart Radio (SLC), CTV via MNTN (inactive), Meta (inactive), Programmatic Display (inactive)
  • Growth brands (store-level Demand Gen): Grease Monkey, Uncle Ed's, American Lube Fast

GM Franchise — Grease Monkey franchisees

  • Funding: NMF (National Marketing Fund) — franchisees contribute ~0.5% of sales
  • Agency: L3 via NMF program. Fee waived for NMF participants. Franchisees can use own agency but pay NMF contribution and agency fee separately.
  • Channels: Google only — Search (Brand + NonBrand), PMax. No Demand Gen, no YouTube, no Meta through NMF.
NMF rate of ~0.5% of sales is an estimate — confirm with finance team.

SD Franchise — SpeeDee franchisees

  • Funding: SAF (SpeeDee Ad Fund) — separate from NMF
  • Agency: L3 for Google. Regional agencies handle other media by market.
  • Google channels: Search (Brand + NonBrand), PMax only
  • Regional agencies:
    • RVPD — Texas / Gulf Coast
    • Quad — NorCal
    • Davis — New England
    • Mid Atlantic — Google only, no agency

ADI — Colorado pooled market

  • What it is: Pooled advertising district covering both ROP and franchise stores in the Colorado area
  • Agency: YouTech (separate from L3)
  • Budget split: 61% NonBrand Search · 27% PMax · ~12% Brand · YouTube (regional)
  • Channels: Google Search (Brand + NonBrand), PMax, Meta, Rapids/Avalanche sports ads via YouTech
Glossary
  • ROP: Corporate-owned store (Run of Program)
  • Zee / Franchisee: Independently owned franchise location
  • NMF: National Marketing Fund — GM franchisee pooled ad contributions (~0.5% of sales)
  • SAF: SpeeDee Ad Fund — SD franchise equivalent of NMF
  • L3: Primary digital agency for ROP and NMF franchisees. 11.5% fee for non-NMF stores.
  • ADI: Colorado pooled ad district — managed by YouTech
  • YouTech: Agency managing ADI (Colorado) campaigns across Google, Meta, and sports/event placements
  • MNTN: CTV (connected TV) vendor — previously used in Atlanta region, not currently active
  • SEM: Search Engine Marketing = SEO (organic) + PPC (paid)
  • CPSV: Cost Per Store Visit — primary metric tying ad spend to physical foot traffic
  • Growth brands: Grease Monkey, Uncle Ed's, American Lube Fast — receive store-level Demand Gen in addition to standard channels
  • CTV: Connected TV advertising — video ads served on streaming platforms via MNTN
